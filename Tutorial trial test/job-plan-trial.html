Centrelink will not process your application if you do not agree to your Job Plan within 28 days of applying for income support. You will not get your income support payment and you could have to apply again.

Why you need a Job Plan

Your Job Plan is an agreement you make with the Australian Government to get an income support payment while you look for a job.

You need a Job Plan to get your income support payment.

  • The main goal of your Job Plan is for you to find and keep a job
  • It requires you to do tasks that are a fair exchange for you to get your income support payment. These are called mutual obligation requirements
  • Your mutual obligation requirements are compulsory. They are not negotiable

Each time you don’t meet your obligations your payment will be suspended and you will get a demerit. It will remain suspended until you do meet your obligations.

You will be removed from the Online Employment Services Trial and referred to an employment services provider if you get 3 demerits.

What you need to do

When you’re in the trial you manage your Job Plan by yourself for up to six months.

You will need to do tasks and report online through your jobactive website account, or the Job Seeker app.

  • Agree to your Job Plan
  • Apply for jobs
  • Report your job search every month
  • Develop your own resume and cover letters

You won’t be connected with an employment services provider while you’re in the trial.

Contact

If you have questions about completing this Job Plan, or if you can't manage yourself online, call the job seeker hotline on 13 62 68.

Please note your phone company could charge you for this call.

Definition of a provider during the online trial

In the online trial your provider is not a person. It is a digital service you access through your jobactive website account or the Job Seeker app. You won’t be referred to any job interviews, activities or appointments by an employment services provider, the jobactive website or the Job Seeker app while you are in the trial.
