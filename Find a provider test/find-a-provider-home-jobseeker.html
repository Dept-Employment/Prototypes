Find a provider

Find a Provider

Not sure which provider is right for you?

Answer a couple of questions to find out which provider is best to help you find and keep a job.


Start questions

Unsure which Provider
jobactive

jobactive is for most people looking for work. It's helped more than 1 million Australians find a job.

What can it do for you?

Get help to write a resume, look for work, prepare for interviews and build your skills. jobactive providers may cover costs for things like work clothes and uniforms, travel costs, driver’s licence, training and other items you need to help you get a job.

Under 25?

Providers can enrol you in Employability Skills Training to help you get the skills employers want. They can help you find a PaTH internship so you can earn a little extra money each week while you show an employer what you can do.

Community Development Programme (CDP)

CDP is for job seekers who live in remote places in Australia.

What can it do for you?

Get real work experience and build your skills doing work in your local community. CDP providers can help you get your driver’s licence and other qualifications that will help you get a job.

Find out more about CDP

Disability Employment Services (DES)

DES is for job seekers with a disability. Different services are available depending on how much support you need to get a job.

Employment Support Services is for people who:

  • have a permanent disability, illness or injury
  • need long-term, regular support to find and keep a job

Disability Management Services is for people who:

  • have a disability, illness or injury
  • need occasional support to find and keep a job

What can it do for you?

DES providers support you while you're getting ready to work and stay with you after you get a job. You can get help with your resume and interview skills, on-the-job training and modifying your workplace to meet your needs.

Find out more about DES

Harvest Labour Services (HLS)

HLS is for people who want to pick fruit and veggies on farms around Australia.

What can it do for you?

Hitting the harvest trail is a great way to earn money while you travel. HLS providers connect you with farmers and producers across Australia. They can also tell you if you’re eligible to earn an extra $5,000 a year without affecting your income support payment.

Explore the harvest trail

ParentsNext

ParentsNext is for people who have a child aged under 6 and are looking to plan for the future.

What can it do for you?

Get personalised help to set work and education goals that work for you and your family. ParentsNext providers connect you with services, opportunities and other people in your local community. Build your skills and gain confidence so when you’re ready to go back to work you’ll hit the ground running.

Find out more about ParentsNext

New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)

NEIS is for job seekers aged 18 or older who want to start a small business.

What can it do for you?

Get professional help to turn your business idea into a reality. NEIS providers give you access to business training and mentoring, as well as income support and rental assistance while you build your business.

Find out more about NEIS

Transition to Work (TtW)

TtW is for young people aged 15 to 21 who need to build skills and confidence to get a job.

A TtW provider can help you if you:

  • haven’t had a job in the last six months
  • don’t have a Year 12 certificate or a Certificate III

What can it do for you?

Get support while you’re getting ready to work and after you get a job. You can do training, get qualifications, learn what employers expect and do some work experience. Transition to Work providers work closely with employers to make sure you land in a job that’s right for you.

Find out more about Transition to Work
