Our Job Seekers

MADEC will provide:

  • All job seekers with an initial face to face interview including a comprehensive Job Readiness Assessment and Introduction to Service, providing information on current vacancies and job search strategies.
  • A Résumé Improvement Service via phone and/or electronic contact ie. skype and email.
  • Access to additional face to face appointments with a Work Pathways Coordinator during the first 6 weeks of service.
  • Minimum 6 monthly re-assessments to ensure service options are current and reflect your individual needs.
  • Access to on-site job search facilities and Wi-Fi at no cost to you during business hours at each site.
  • Unlimited access to our online self-help resource centre which contains practical guides, tools and templates, frequently asked questions, links to service agencies and job search websites to assist you to find a job.
  • Activities to meet your mutual obligations in conjunction with the Work for the Dole Coordinator and other support organisations that will provide a work-like culture.
  • Customised support to you including specialist Youth and Indigenous Consultants where relevant.
  • Services delivered with the support from Indigenous Coordinators/communities in culturally safe locations

Our Employers

Labour market needs and networks

MADEC will:

  • Work with peak industry bodies, employers, employment providers, support agencies and training providers to expand on our current regional knowledge and develop Regional Labour Market Plans to identify areas of employment growth and skill shortage and respond to the needs of the region.
  • Work with Group Training Organisations and Australian Apprenticeship Support Networks to provide you with up to date information and access to these services.
  • Provide an induction card service to harvest employers to allow for identification of eligible workers.
  • Deliver Industry Specific Work First Program developed to address local employer needs in priority industry areas, matching job seeker skills to your vacancies.

Employer support

MADEC will:

  • Ensure you have access to Business Development teams and Employment Brokers.
  • Provide you with direct contact with an Employment Broker to ensure customer relationships are effectively managed.
  • Provide an introductory MADEC Service Booklet tailored to the region and industry area, including information on employment services, wage subsidies and incentives.
  • Work with you to identify your skill and labour requirements allowing a full understanding of your business needs to provide you with a MADEC Service Offer.
  • Draw on labour hire and training capability to provide you with competitive services that meet your ongoing requirements.
  • Offer assistance to induct, orientate and provide ongoing support to a new employee.
  • Work with other employment services providers to develop strategies to ensure you have access to work ready job seekers.
  • Provide you with the opportunity to attend and meet with potential employees during pre-employment activities.

Our Networks

MADEC completes a Regional Labour Market Profile to ensure that we are aware of employers need and opportunities for job seekers within the area.

We participate in provider network meetings with other local employment providers to discuss issues, identify opportunities and collaborate on employment solutions. It is at these meetings that forecasted labour need is identified and providers are able to work together to ensure employers have access to the most appropriate applicants.

As a result of MADEC's presence at these meetings, MADEC has partnered with external training providers to develop and deliver industry specific training to meet employers needs and strengthen opportunities for our job seekers

MADEC collaborates with other employment providers when specific training has been developed to address industry skills gaps to ensure a larger pool of work-ready job seekers are available. This strategy has proven effective for smaller remote communities who do not have ready access to training opportunities.

Additionally MADEC understands support services delivered in each Region, we offer our job seekers Social Work services and assist job seekers engage with these programs to overcome barriers, and prepare job seekers for employment

Our Performances

MADEC is a successful not-for-profit organisation with a proud 45 year history of service delivery throughout Victoria, New South Wales and South Australia.

MADEC has the capacity to identify vacancies through external avenues and also MADECs Labour Hire, National Harvest Labour Information Services and Harvest Labour Services. Through our employment services MADEC has been able to move 68% of our job seekers into work.  

MADEC strives to support Indigenous job seekers to gain and sustain employment, we are proud of exceeding outcomes for Indigenous job seekers.

MADEC is the provider of the Governments National Harvest Labour Information Service (NHLIS). For the past 10 years the MADEC NHLIS has mobilised workers and co-ordinated labour supply to meet employer needs in the horticulture industry across Australia. The NHLIS facilitates the listing and filling of over 10,000 vacancies per year for employers.

MADEC is the largest Harvest Labour Service provider in Australia delivering services Victoria, New South Wales and South Australia. In the last 2 years MADEC has placed a total of 15,211 workers into harvest work and has established a database of over 8,600 employer (grower) contacts. MADEC is also one of the largest employers in the Government Seasonal Worker Program supplying employers across Australia with over 250 workers per year from overseas.

MADEC is a nationally Registered Training Organisation (RTO) and delivers skill based training to more than 7,000 students per year. MADEC has 25 years experience working with employers through formal industry consultation to understand their skill requirements.  MADEC is able to draw on more than 50 accredited courses to design training that is aligned to regional workforce priorities to meet employer and job seeker needs.

MADEC is also a successful Labour Hire provider working with 170 employers to fill more than 1,200 vacancies per year in various occupations.

About Us

MADEC is a successful not-for-profit organisation that has a proud 45 year history of service delivery throughout Victoria, New South Wales and South Australia. Employing approximately 300 staff, MADEC is contracted to provide jobactive services across 6 employment regions, Disability Management Service, operate 7 Harvest Labour Offices, manage the National Harvest Labour Information Service. Labour Hire provider and deliver Indigenous Youth Leadership Programs and Indigenous Community Links (ICL).

MADEC is a nationally Registered Training Organisation (RTO) delivering accredited training to more than 7,000 students per annum. MADEC also provides Labour Hire services in partnership with industry and is an approved provider of the Australian Government Seasonal Worker Program.
